How we got here

Starting from what it needs to prove

The Bloc's documentation on the SETS Engine is mostly a validation plan, a test matrix of what the tool needs to demonstrate before it can be trusted. That plan doesn't describe the engine. It describes what happens when the engine fails.

We worked backwards from those tests. A tool that needs to "correctly respond when a single element is modified" has to understand what that element does in the asset. A tool that needs to "align with human expert judgment" has to evaluate along dimensions experts would actually use. A tool that needs to "apply rules consistently" needs explicit, deterministic logic. Not AI inference that varies run to run.

What follows is our read of what the engine has to be in order to pass its own tests: six scoring dimensions, weights that shift by context, a set of hard rules, and a clear output format. This is a proposed definition, not a description of what's been built. Use it to get the team aligned before validation starts.

Architecture

The 6 scoring agents

Each agent has its own domain, scoring criteria, and evidence requirements. All six run at the same time; a validation layer then checks for contradictions between them before anything is surfaced.

01
Regulatory & Compliance

FDA and regulatory requirements

Checks the structural and legal requirements: ISI presence and completeness, fair balance, required safety disclosures, compliant claims language. Failures here are not stylistic. They are legal. This is the only dimension where a bad score can stop an asset regardless of everything else.

ISI presence Fair balance Safety disclosures Claims language Required disclaimers
Hard blocker? Yes
Blocker threshold Score < 40 flags overall result regardless of other dimensions
02
Scientific Accuracy

Clinical and medical content integrity

Separate from regulatory: this one asks whether the claims are actually true. Substantiated efficacy language, correct statistics, no off-label implications, data presented in context. An asset can clear every compliance checkbox and still mislead. This is what catches that.

Claims substantiation Efficacy language Statistical accuracy Off-label risk Data context
Hard blocker? Yes
Blocker threshold Score < 40 flags overall result regardless of other dimensions
03
Health Literacy

Accessibility and reading level

Patient materials should hit 8th grade reading level or below. HCP materials can go higher but still need to avoid unnecessary jargon. This agent scores plain language, terminology choices, and whether the medical concepts are actually accessible to the person reading them.

Reading level Plain language Medical jargon Concept accessibility Audience calibration
Hard blocker? No
Note Threshold varies by audience: patient vs. HCP assessed differently
04
Messaging & Strategy

Strategic clarity and differentiation

Is the core message clear? Does the value proposition land? Does the asset say something specific about the brand, or could it be for any drug in the category? This is the strategic layer: what the asset is saying, not how it looks or whether it gets someone to click.

Message clarity Value proposition Brand differentiation Single-minded focus Audience relevance
Hard blocker? No
Note Requires campaign objective as context input to score meaningfully
05
Engagement

Motivation and behavioral resonance

Does the asset move someone to do something? CTA presence, clarity, urgency. Emotional tone relative to the therapeutic area. A strategically solid asset can still be emotionally flat. This dimension is where that shows up. Informing the audience and motivating them are not the same thing.

CTA presence CTA clarity Emotional tone Behavioral motivation Urgency
Hard blocker? No
Rule trigger No CTA present = automatic penalty on this dimension
06
Visual & Clarity

Craft, hierarchy, and readability

Is there too much copy for the format? Does the eye know where to go first? Is the imagery doing real work or just filling space? Font size, contrast, spacing. A strategically strong asset can still fall apart at this level. That is what the craft review catches.

Copy volume Visual hierarchy Imagery quality Readability Format appropriateness
Hard blocker? No
Rule trigger Stock-heavy imagery = flag; minimal copy for dense format = flag
Scoring System

Score categories & thresholds

Each agent scores 0–100, mapped to four categories. The overall score is a weighted average across all six. Regulatory and Scientific Accuracy are hard blockers. A score below 40 on either flags the overall result no matter how the other four dimensions perform.

0–39
Poor
Significant issues requiring immediate attention. If Regulatory or Scientific, a hard block on the asset.
40–59
Adequate
Meets minimum standards but falls short of best practice. Improvement recommended before deployment.
60–79
Good
Above standard. Minor improvements available but asset is deployable as-is.
80–100
Strong
Best-in-class execution. Use as a benchmark reference for this asset type.
Weighting Model

Context-sensitive weights

Rule Logic

Hard rules & automatic triggers

On top of the dimensional scores, the engine has a set of deterministic rules: specific conditions that trigger automatic penalties. Some things in pharma marketing are just wrong, and a good score on five other dimensions doesn't change that.

No CTA present

No CTA means no intended next action. Automatic penalty on Engagement. The score on that dimension cannot reach Good, regardless of how well everything else reads.

Missing ISI

Patient-facing print and broadcast assets without Important Safety Information get a Regulatory hard block. The dimension floors at Poor and the overall result is flagged. Full stop.

Stock-heavy imagery

Heavy reliance on generic stock photography gets flagged on Visual & Clarity. Not a hard fail, but the maximum achievable score on that dimension drops to Adequate.

Minimal copy in dense formats

Detail aids or print ads that make claims without enough copy to support them get flagged on both Messaging and Scientific Accuracy. If you can't back it up in the asset, it's a substantiation gap.

High engagement + poor regulatory

A cross-agent check: if Engagement scores 70 or above while Regulatory is below 40, a warning fires. A compelling asset with compliance failures is more dangerous than a weak one. It is more likely to reach someone.

Reading level mismatch

Patient-facing assets that score above 8th grade reading level get flagged. Not a hard block, but it comes up first in the priority fixes: it is the kind of thing that gets missed in review and then matters a lot in the real world.

Inputs

What the engine needs to score

The engine takes three inputs alongside the asset. Without them, scoring gets less accurate and some dimensions can't run properly. The agents need context to know what they're judging against.

🎯

Therapeutic area

The disease state or treatment category. Regulatory expectations, acceptable claims language, and literacy assumptions all change depending on this. The Regulatory and Scientific agents need it to run.

👤

Target audience

Patient, HCP, caregiver, payer. This determines which weight profile applies and sets the reading level and tone expectations. Health Literacy and Engagement both need it to score correctly.

📋

Campaign objective

Awareness, conversion, education, adherence: what is this asset actually supposed to do? The Messaging & Strategy agent needs this to tell whether the asset was built for the right goal in the first place.

Outputs

What the engine produces

Overall score (0–100)
Weighted average across all six dimensions, mapped to Poor / Adequate / Good / Strong. The headline result.
Dimension scores × 6
Individual 0–100 score for each agent with category label. Shows where the asset is strong and where it fails.
Evidence quotes
Each agent returns a direct quote or element from the asset that most influenced its score. Grounds the score in the actual material.
Priority fixes
Top 5 recommendations ranked by severity. Lowest-scoring dimension issues surface first. Specific and actionable, not generic.
Inline annotations
Flagged elements tagged as error, warning, or strength, tied to the dimension that identified them. Enables visual overlay on the asset itself.
Consistency warnings
Cross-agent contradictions surfaced separately (e.g. high engagement with poor regulatory compliance). Alerts the reviewer to systemic issues.
Hard block flags
Explicit alert if Regulatory or Scientific Accuracy fall below 40. Overrides overall score display. The asset cannot be considered passable regardless of other dimensions.
Validation Program

How this definition gets tested

The definition above is a proposed standard. Before the engine can be used with any real confidence, it has to be validated across five dimensions. The full test matrix is in the separate Validation Plan. These are the questions each dimension is trying to answer:

Precision
Is the tool consistent when run repeatedly?
Score variance · Pass consistency · Dimension stability
Accuracy
Does it agree with human expert judgment?
Expert agreement · Category alignment · Dimension accuracy · Recommendation match
Range
Does it use the full scale without clustering?
Benchmark ladder · Forced contrast · Score distribution · Rank ordering
Robustness
Does it hold up under minor irrelevant changes?
Format variation · Metadata variation · Noise injection · Edge cases · Uptime
Specificity
Does it correctly identify what's wrong and why?
Single-factor manipulation · Rule triggers · Recommendation relevance · False positive/negative
Hedgehox Feedback

On the benchmark question:
don't collect. Let teams contribute.

The current validation plan requires 500–1,000 assets before the engine can be properly tested. That's a lot of work before beta even starts, and it assumes someone centrally has the time and therapeutic-area expertise to curate assets across every format and indication. We don't think that's the right starting point.

Skip the collection phase entirely.
Let the beta program build the benchmark.

The idea

Each team in the beta gets an upload portal for their own final-approved assets. Work that's already cleared medical, legal, regulatory, and client review becomes the benchmark (not a curated dataset, but The Bloc's own proven output).

Why it's a better signal

Final-approved assets have already passed every review gate. That is a stronger quality signal than asking a few experts to score something on demand. A cardiovascular team's approved work is the right benchmark for cardiovascular work. It's specific in a way a generic pharma standard can't be.

It gets better as you use it

Every upload makes the benchmark richer. The engine is useful from day one with a small corpus and improves as teams keep uploading. The agency ends up building institutional knowledge as a side effect of using the tool, not as a prerequisite for it.

The scoring model becomes three layers instead of two: the 6 agents do dimensional analysis, a knowledge base retrieval layer pulls the most relevant approved assets as comparators, and the output shows both an absolute score and a score relative to comparable approved work from the same team. The 500–1,000 asset requirement stops being a hard prerequisite and becomes something the beta program builds naturally.
